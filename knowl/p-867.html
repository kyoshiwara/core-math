Another common ERROR is to think that 0.058 is larger than 0.4, because "zero doesn't count." But in this case the zero tells us that

\begin{equation*} 0.\alert{0}58~~~~~~\text{has}~~~~~~\alert{0}~\text{tenths} \end{equation*}

while

\begin{equation*} 0.\alert{4}~~~~~~\text{has}~~~~~~\alert{4}~\text{tenths} \end{equation*}

Once again, we compare decimal fractions by comparing each place value in order.
