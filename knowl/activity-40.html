Activity 3.4.4. Quilt Patterns.

This quilt pattern is called Drunkard's Path. The entire block (without the border) measures 30 centimeters on each side, and it is made out of 16 patches.

  1. Find the dimensions and area of each patch.

  2. Each patch includes a quarter of a circle, and the radius of that quarter-circle is four-fifths of the side of the patch.

    1. Find the radius and area of one quarter-circle.

    2. Find the area of one blue piece.

  3. What is the total area of the blue pieces needed for one quilt block?

This quilt is called Birdhouse Row. Each birdhouse sits in a square that measures \(6\dfrac{1}{2}\) inches on a side.

  1. Find the area of one square.

  2. The blue roof of the birdhouse is \(1\dfrac{1}{2}\) inches wide. Find the area of one birdhouse roof.

  3. The small patch of background color at the bottom of the birdhouse is half a square with side length \(1\dfrac{1}{2}\) inches. Find the area of this triangle.

  4. The door to each birdhouse is a circle of diameter \(1\dfrac{1}{2}\) inches. Find the area of one door.

  5. The rest of the front of each birdhouse is red. What is the total area of the red pieces in the quilt?
