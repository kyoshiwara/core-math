Challenge Problem.

The radius of the earth is approximately 3963 miles. Suppose you have a string stretched tight around the equator of the earth.

  1. How long is the string?

  2. Now suppose you add 10 feet to the length of the string. How long is it now (in feet)?

  3. Stretch out your new string so that it forms a circle a little bit bigger than the equator. What is the radius of the new circle?

  4. How high above the surface of the earth does the new circle of string sit?
