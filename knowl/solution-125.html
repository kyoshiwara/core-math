Solution 6.2.1.1.

To compute the tuition, we multiply the number of units by 30 and add 400 to the result. For example, if you take 6 units, your tuition is
\begin{equation*} (6 \times 30) + 400 = 580 \end{equation*}
We make a table showing several pairs of variables and compute their ratios.

of Units		 Tuition \(\dfrac{\text{Tuition}}{\text{Units}}\)

The ratio \(\dfrac{\text{tuition}}{\text{units}}\text{,}\) or tuition per unit, is not constant, so the tuition is not proportional to the number of units.
