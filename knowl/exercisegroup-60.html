For Problems 19-24, use letters to represent the variable quantities.
19.
Yasmin needs \(9 \dfrac{1}{2}\) hours per week to study for her math class.
  1. If Yasmin has 15 study hours per week, how many hours does she have for her other classes?
  2. Explain how to find the number of study hours Yasmin has for her other classes if she has \(t\) hours of weekly study time.
  3. Fill in the table for the given values of \(t\text{.}\) (Hint: First subtract 9 from the value of \(t\text{,}\) then subtract \(\dfrac{1}{2}\) from the result.)
    \(t \hphantom{000}\) 16 \(\hphantom{000}\) 20 \(\hphantom{000}\) 25 \(\hphantom{000}\) 31 \(\hphantom{000}\)
    \(t - 9\dfrac{1}{2}\)
20.
Rose and her two sisters, Iris and Violet, plan to sell the family farm and move to Florida. They will divide the money from the sale equally.
  1. How much will Rose get if they make $30,000 on the sale?
  2. Explain how to find Rose's share if they make \(s\) dollars.
  3. Fill in the table for the given values of \(s\text{.}\)
    \(s \hphantom{000}\) 36,000 45,000 51,000 114,000
    \(\dfrac{s}{3}\)
21.
Kranberry Kooler is 10% cranberry juice.
  1. How much cranberry juice is in 15 quarts of Kranberry Kooler?
  2. Explain how to find the number of quarts of cranberry juice needed for \(q\) quarts of Kranberry Kooler.
  3. Fill in the table for the given values of \(q\text{.}\)
    \(q \hphantom{000}\) 20 45 260 1250
    \(0.10 \times q\)
22.

  1. If the English department has 5200 students enrolled this year, how many should they expect next year?
  2. Explain how a department with \(p\) students this year can predict next year's enrollment.
  3. Fill in the table for the given values of \(p\text{.}\)
    \(p \hphantom{000}\) 600 1300 2200 3500
    \(1.04 \times p\)
23.

  1. How long will Sinead's hair be after 30 days?
  2. Explain how to find the length of Sinead's hair after \(t\) days.
  3. Fill in the table for the given values of \(t\text{.}\)
    \(t \hphantom{000}\) \(\hphantom{0}\)7\(\hphantom{0}\) 60\(\hphantom{0}\) 200 360
    \(0.085\times t\)
