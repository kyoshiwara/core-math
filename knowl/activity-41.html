Activity 3.5.1. How Big Is a Billion?

Here are three ways to think about the size of one billion.
  1. Suppose you want to count to one billion, saying one number per second.
    1. How many minutes will it take you to count to 1000?
    2. How many minutes will it take you to count to one million? How long is that in days?
    3. How many days will it take you to count to one billion? How long is that in years?
  2. A stack of 100 one-dollar bills is 0.43 inches or 1.1 centimeters high.
    1. How high is a stack of 1000 one-dollar bills, in inches?
    2. How high is a stack of one million one-dollar bills, in inches? How high is that in feet?
    3. How high is a stack of one billion one-dollar bills, in feet? How high is that in miles?
  3. The length of a one-dollar bill is 6.14 inches.
    1. How long is a string of 1000 one-dollar bills, laid end to end? How far is that in feet?
    2. How long is a string of one million one-dollar bills, in feet? How far is that in miles?
    3. How long is a string of one billion one-dollar bills, in miles?
    4. The distance around the Earth is 24,880 miles. How many times would a string of one billion one-dollar bills circle the Earth?
