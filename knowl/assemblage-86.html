Circles and Spheres.

The diameter of a circle is twice its radius.
\begin{equation*} \blert{D = 2 \times r} \end{equation*}
Circumference of a circle
\begin{equation*} \blert{C = \pi \times D} \hphantom{0000} \text {or} \hphantom{0000} \blert{C = 2 \times \pi \times r} \end{equation*}
Area of a circle
\begin{equation*} \blert{A = \pi \times r^2} \end{equation*}
Volume of a sphere
\begin{equation*} \blert{\text{Volume } = \dfrac{4}{3} \times \pi r^3} \end{equation*}
