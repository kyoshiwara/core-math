Note 7.1.13.

We have been using parentheses to enclose the signed numbers in a sum. The parentheses help us decide which way the symbols and are used:
  • to indicate a positive or negative number, or
  • to show addition or subtraction.
Also, it can be confusing to have two plus or minus symbols in a row.
However, we can often leave out the parentheses if it is easy to interpret the expression. For instance, the three sums in Checkpoint 7.1.12 can be written as follows.
  1. \(\displaystyle -2 + (-2)\)
  2. \(\displaystyle -4 + 3\)
  3. \(\displaystyle 6 + (-8)\)
