Solution 6.2.12.1.

  1. We read the ounces and cents values for each of the three points. The standard containers are 10 ounces for $3.00, 16 ounces for $4.80, and 24 ounces for $7.20.
  2. To find the unit cost, we compute the ratio of cost to ounces.
    \begin{equation*} \dfrac{\text{300 cents}}{\text{10 ounces}} = \text{30 cents per ounce} \end{equation*}
  3. 25 ounces would cost
    \begin{equation*} \text{(25 ounces)} \times \text{(30 cents per ounce)} = \text{750 cents} \end{equation*}
    So we would need to extend the vertical axis by another 30 units, to 750.
