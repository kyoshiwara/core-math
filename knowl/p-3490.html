  • Do not confuse powers with products! For example, \(3^5\) does not mean \(3 \times 5\text{.}\) Remember that an exponent tells us how many times the base occurs in a repeated multiplication.
  • Is \(3^5\) the same as \(5^3\) ? No!
    \begin{align*} 3^5 \amp = 3 \cdot 3 \cdot 3 \cdot 3 \cdot 3 = 243 \amp \blert{\text{Five factors of 3}}\\ 5^3 \amp = 5 \cdot 5 \cdot 5 = 125 \amp \blert{\text{Three factors of 5}} \end{align*}
