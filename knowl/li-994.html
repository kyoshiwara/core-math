Item.

To find \(\dfrac{2}{3}\) of a number, we first find \(\dfrac{1}{3}\) of the number (divide by 3), then multiply the result by 2:

\begin{equation*} \dfrac{2}{3}~ \text{of}~ 36 = 2 \times 12 = 24 \end{equation*}

Exercise

  1. Find \(\dfrac{1}{4}\) of 120.

  2. Find \(\dfrac{3}{4}\) of 120.
