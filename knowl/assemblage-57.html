A Quick Refresher.

Recall that we can find the area of a rectangle by multiplying its length times its width. In the figures below, the grid lines represent centimeters.

\begin{gather*} \hphantom{Area = 6 \times 4 = 24}\\ \text{Area} = 6 \times 4 = 24~~ \text{square centimeters} \end{gather*}

We can find the volume of a box by multiplying its length times its width times its height.

\begin{align*} \text{Volume} \amp = 6 \times 4 \times 3\\ \amp = 72~~ \text{cubic centimeters} \end{align*}
