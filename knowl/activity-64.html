Activity 6.1.1. Comparing Quantities.

  1. Jared and Carrie joined their department’s "Losing It" contest for slimming down and getting in shape. Here are their weights before and after the six-month trial.
    Before After
    Jared 210 lbs 190 lbs
    Carrie 135 lbs 120 lbs
    1. Use additive comparison to compute each person’s weight loss in pounds.
    2. Use multiplicative comparison to compute each person’s weight loss as a percentage of their initial weight.
    3. Describe what each type of comparison tells you about Jared’s and Carrie’s weight loss.
    4. Which person do you think did better in the contest? Why?
  2. You are considering two different jobs, each of which will pay you $20,000 in your first year and $21,000 in your second year.
    1. Use additive comparison to calculate the change in your salary after one year. If Job A calculates your new salary the same way each year, how much will you make in your third year? Your fourth year? Record your answers in the table below.
