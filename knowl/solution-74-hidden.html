First we'll write \(3\dfrac{3}{4}\) as an improper fraction. Remember that \(3\dfrac{3}{4}\) means \(\dfrac{3}{1} + \dfrac{3}{4}\text{.}\)
\begin{equation*} \dfrac{3}{1} + \dfrac{3}{4} = \left(\dfrac{3}{1} \times \dfrac{\alert{4}}{\alert{4}} \right) + \dfrac{3}{4} = \dfrac{12}{4} + \dfrac{3}{4} = \dfrac{15}{4} ~~~~~~ \blert{\text{Build, then add.}} \end{equation*}
To divide \(\dfrac{15}{4}\) by \(\dfrac{3}{8}\text{,}\) we multiply \(\dfrac{15}{4}\) by \(\dfrac{8}{3}\text{.}\)
\begin{align*} \dfrac{15}{4} \div \dfrac{3}{8} \amp = \dfrac{15}{4} \times \dfrac{8}{3} = \dfrac{15 \times 8}{4 \times 3} ~~~~~~ \blert{\dfrac{\text{Multiply numerators together.}}{\text{Multiply denominators together.}}}\\ \amp = \dfrac{120}{12} = 10 \end{align*}
So \(3\dfrac{3}{4}\) can be broken into 10 pieces of size \(\dfrac{3}{8}\text{.}\)
