1. To convert 100 meters to yards, we multiply the length of 1 meter by 100.
    \begin{equation*} 100 \text{ meters} = 100 \times 1.0963 \text{ yards} = 109.63 \text{ yards} \end{equation*}
    And because there are 3 feet in a yard,
    \begin{equation*} 100 \text{ meters} = 109.63 \cancel{\text{ yard}} \times \dfrac{3 \text{ feet}}{1 \cancel{\text{ yard}}} = 328.89 \text{ feet} \end{equation*}
  2. Usain Bolt's speed was
    \begin{equation*} \dfrac{328.89 \text{ feet}}{9.58 \text{ seconds}} = 34.33 \text{ feet per second} \end{equation*}
