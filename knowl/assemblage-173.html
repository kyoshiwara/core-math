A Quick Refresher.

Remember that to make an equivalent fraction we must multiply or divide top and bottom by the same number. So, for example,
\begin{equation*} \dfrac{3}{2}~~ \text{is equivalent to} ~~ \dfrac{3 \times 5}{2 \times 5} = \dfrac{15}{10} \end{equation*}
We cannot add or subtract the same number to get an equivalent fraction. For example,
\begin{equation*} \dfrac{4}{3}~~ \text{is not equivalent to} ~~ \dfrac{4 + 1}{3 + 1} = \dfrac{5}{4} \end{equation*}
