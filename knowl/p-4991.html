Multiplying by \(\dfrac{1}{3}\) also works to find \(\dfrac{1}{3}\) of a dozen eggs, if we write 12 as a fraction, \(\dfrac{12}{1}\text{.}\)
\begin{equation*} \dfrac{1}{3} \times \dfrac{12}{1} = \dfrac{1 \times 12}{3 \times 1} = \dfrac{12}{3} =4 ~~~~~~\blert{\dfrac{\text{Multiply the numerators together.}}{\text{Multiply the denominators together.}}} \end{equation*}
