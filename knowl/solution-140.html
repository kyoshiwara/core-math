Solution 6.4.6.1.

First we find the scale factor.
\begin{equation*} 24 \times \text{scale factor} = 32, ~~~ \text{so} ~~~ \text{scale factor} = \dfrac{32}{24} \end{equation*}
In this case we can reduce the scale factor, dividing top and bottom by 8.
\begin{equation*} \dfrac{32}{24} = \dfrac{32 \div 8}{24 \div 8} = \dfrac{4}{3} \end{equation*}
To find \(H\text{,}\) we multiply the corresponding side of the small triangle by the scale factor.
\begin{equation*} 9 \times \dfrac{4}{3} = \dfrac{36}{3} = 12 \end{equation*}
The length, \(H\text{,}\) of the vertical side of the large triangle is 12 feet.
\(\blert{\text{Check:}}\) Here are the ratios of the sides, large triangle to small triangle:
\begin{gather*} \text{Horizontal sides:} ~~~ \dfrac{36}{24}\\ \text{Vertical sides:} ~~~ \dfrac{12}{9} \end{gather*}
You can verify that both of these ratios reduce to \(\dfrac{4}{3}\text{,}\) the scale factor.
