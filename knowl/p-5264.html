If you know about negative numbers, you will have noticed that 5 is not the only square root of 25. We can see that \(-5\) is another square root of 25, because

\begin{equation*} (-5)^2 = (-5) \cdot (-5) = 25 \end{equation*}

In fact, every positive number has two square roots, one positive and one negative. We'll learn more about negative numbers in the next lesson.
