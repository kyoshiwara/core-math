The scale factor of the dimensions is the ratio of the Lilliputian's height to the human's height:
\begin{equation*} \dfrac{6~\text{inches}}{6~\text{feet}} = \dfrac{6~\text{inches}}{72~\text{inches}} = \dfrac{1}{12} \end{equation*}
Thus, the volume is scaled by
\begin{equation*} \left(\dfrac{1}{12}\right)^3 = \dfrac{1}{12} \times \dfrac{1}{12} \times \dfrac{1}{12} = \dfrac{1}{1728} \end{equation*}
The volume of a Lilliputian would be \(\frac{1}{1728}\) of the volume of a 6-foot tall human. (The weight of a Lilliputian would also be \(\frac{1}{1728}\) of the weight of a human. A 6-foot tall man weighs about 180 pounds, so a Lilliputian would weigh \(\frac{180}{1728}\) pounds, or \(1\frac{2}{3}\) ounces.)
