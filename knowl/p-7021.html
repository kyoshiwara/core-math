  1. If Uncle Herb has 700 calories for lunch, how many calories can he have for dinner?
  2. Write an equation for the number of calories, \(d\text{,}\) Uncle Herb can have for dinner if he consumes \(c\) calories for lunch.
  3. Fill in the table for the given values of \(c\text{.}\)
    \(c\) 200 400 600 800 1000
    \(d\)
  4. Plot the points from the table, and connect them with a straight line. Extend your line to the edges of the grid.
