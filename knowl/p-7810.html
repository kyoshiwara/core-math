  1. We start by making a table that shows the temperature, \(T\text{,}\) at \(h\) hours after noon. For every hour that passes, we subtract 2 degrees.
    \(~~ h~~\) 0 2 5 9 12
    \(T\) 18 14 8 0 -6
    Then we plot the points from the table on a grid, as shown at right. Finally, we connect the points with a straight line.
  2. To compute the slope of the line, we choose two points on the graph, say \((4,12)\) and \((9,2)\text{.}\) Now the the output variable, \(T\text{,}\) decreases by 10 degrees (from 12 degrees to 2 degrees), so we say that \(\Delta T = -10\text{.}\) The input variable, \(h\text{,}\) increases from 4 to 9, so \(\Delta h = 5\text{.}\) Next we calculate the slope ratio.
    \begin{equation*} \dfrac{\Delta T}{\Delta h} = \dfrac{-10 ~\text{degrees}}{5 ~\text{hours}} = -2 ~\text{degrees/hour} \end{equation*}
  3. The slope is \(-2\text{.}\) It represents the rate at which the temperature is decreasing, 2 degrees per hour.
