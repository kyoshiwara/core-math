Solution 3.3.4.1.

We'll use a conversion factor. Because 1 foot = 12 inches, the two fractions

\begin{equation*} \dfrac{12 \text{ inches}}{1 \text{ foot}}~~~~~~\text{and}~~~~~~\dfrac{1 \text{ foot}}{12 \text{ inches}} \end{equation*}

are both equal to 1. We can multiply 90 inches by either fraction without changing its value. We'd like to eliminate inches and end up with feet, so we choose the second factor.

\begin{equation*} \dfrac{90\cancel{\text{ inches}}}{1} \times \dfrac{1\text{ foot}}{12 \cancel{\text{ inches}}} = \dfrac{90 \times 1}{1 \times 12} \text{ feet} \end{equation*}

Notice how the "inches" cancel out, leaving us with "feet" as the unit. And we also see that we should divide 90 by 12.

\begin{equation*} \dfrac{90}{12} \text{ feet} = 7.5 \text{ feet} \end{equation*}

Yao Ming is 7.5 feet, or 7 feet 6 inches, tall.
