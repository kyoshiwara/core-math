Solution 5.5.5.1.

  1. Peter will always be 4 years older than Karen.
    Karen's age, \(K\) 0 2 5 9
    Peter's age, \(P\) 4 6 9 13
  2. For this equation, \(K\) is the input variable, and \(P\) is the output variable. Peter's age is 4 more than Karen's age, or
    \begin{equation*} P = K + 4 \end{equation*}
    We start with a value of \(K\text{,}\) and then we add 4 to find the value of \(P\text{.}\)
    \begin{equation*} \blert{\text{Value of}~K \rightarrow~ \text{add 4} \rightarrow~ \text{Value of}~P} \end{equation*}
