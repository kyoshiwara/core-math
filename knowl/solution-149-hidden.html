1. Because \(-4\) and \(-12\) have the same sign, we add their unsigned parts to get
    \begin{equation*} 4 + 12 = 16 \end{equation*}
    We use the same sign, \(-\text{,}\) for the answer. Thus,
    \begin{equation*} \blert{(-4) + (-12) = -16} \end{equation*}
  2. Because \(-8\) and \(+2\) have opposite signs, we subtract their unsigned parts to get
    \begin{equation*} 8 - 2 = 6 \end{equation*}
    The negative number is farther from zero, so the answer is negative. Thus,
    \begin{equation*} \blert{(-8) + (+2) = -6} \end{equation*}
  3. Because \(-7\) and \(+9\) have opposite signs, we subtract their unsigned parts to get
    \begin{equation*} 9 - 7 = 2 \end{equation*}
    The positive number is farther from zero, so the answer is positive.
    \begin{equation*} \blert{(-7) + (+9) = +2} \end{equation*}
