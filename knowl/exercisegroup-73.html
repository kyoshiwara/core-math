Exercise Group 2.5.6.13–14.

For Problems 13-14, choose the equation that best describes each situation. In each case, \(p\) represents the unknown quantity.
\(~~~~~p+8 = 40~~~~~~~~p-8 = 40~~~~~~~~8p=40\)
13.
  1. Eight times a number is 40. What is the number?
  2. A number decreased by 8 is 40. What is the number?
  3. Eight less than a number is 40. What is the number?
  4. Forty exceeds a number by 8. What is the number?
14.
  1. Antoinette scored 8 points more than Katy scored. If Antoinette scored 40 points, how many points did Katy score?
  2. Raul got a discount of $8 on his calculator and paid $40. How much is the regular price?
  3. Eight tickets to the rodeo cost $40. How much does one ticket cost?
  4. If Jaye’s golden retriever puppy gains 8 pounds, it will weigh 40 pounds. How much does the puppy weigh now?
