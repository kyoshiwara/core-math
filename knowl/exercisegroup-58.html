Exercise Group 2.2.8.61–64.

For Problems 61-64, remember that rotating through one whole circle sweeps out an angle of 360°. A sector of a circle is a wedge with its point at the center of the circle.
61.
  1. What is the degree measure of each sector of the circle shown at right?
  2. Shade a sector that shows \(\dfrac{2}{3}\) of the circle. What is the degree measure of that sector?
62.
  1. What is the degree measure of each sector of the circle shown at right?
  2. Shade a sector that shows \(\dfrac{5}{8}\) of the circle. What is the degree measure of that sector?
63.
  1. What is the degree measure of each sector of the circle shown at right?
  2. Shade a sector that shows \(\dfrac{5}{6}\) of the circle. What is the degree measure of that sector?
64.
  1. What is the degree measure of each sector of the circle shown at right?
  2. Shade a sector that shows \(\dfrac{3}{8}\) of the circle. What is the degree measure of that sector?
