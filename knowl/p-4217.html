It is a fact that the circumference of every circle is a little more than three times its diameter. More precisely,

\begin{equation*} \blert{\text{Circumference} = \pi \times \text{diameter}} \end{equation*}

The Greek letter \(\blert{\pi}\) (pi, read "pie") stands for an irrational number:

\begin{equation*} \blert{\pi = 3.141592654 ...} \end{equation*}

We can state this result as a formula for the circumference of a circle.
