Solution 1.1.9.1.

To find \(\dfrac{1}{2}\) of 12, we divide by 2:
\begin{equation*} \dfrac{1}{2}~ \text{of}~ 12 = 12 \div 2 = \alert{6} \end{equation*}
To find \(\dfrac{1}{4}\) of 12, we divide by 4:
\begin{equation*} \dfrac{1}{4}~ \text{of}~ 12 = 12 \div 4 = \alert{3} \end{equation*}
To find \(\dfrac{3}{4}\) of 12, we multiply \(\dfrac{1}{4}\) of 12 by 3:
\begin{equation*} \dfrac{3}{4}~ \text{of}~ 12 = 3 \times 3 = \alert{9} \end{equation*}
