Solution 6.1.13.1.

  1. The ratio of distance to time is
    \begin{equation*} \dfrac{\text{distance}}{\text{time}} = \dfrac{\text{130 miles}}{\text{2.5 hours}} \end{equation*}
    To simplify the ratio, we divide 130 by 2.5 to find
    \begin{equation*} \dfrac{\text{130 miles}}{\text{2.5 hours}} = \dfrac{52}{1} \dfrac{\text{miles}}{\text{hour}} \end{equation*}
  2. We can interpret the simplified ratio as "52 miles per hour." It gives us the average speed at which Kayla traveled. For each hour that elapsed, Kayla traveled 52 miles.
