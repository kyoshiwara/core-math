In part (b) of the Example, we used multiplication to compare Boris' and Cyril's weights: Boris weighs \(3\) times as much as Cyril, or
\begin{equation*} \blert{\dfrac{\text{Boris' weight}}{\text{Cyril's weight}} = \dfrac{30}{10} = 3} \end{equation*}
We use division to make a multiplicative comparison. For the weight-lifters,
\begin{equation*} \blert{\dfrac{\text{Boris' weight}}{\text{Cyril's weight}} = \dfrac{270}{250} = 1.08} \end{equation*}
Boris weighs \(1.08\) times as much as Cyril. (We can also say that Boris weighs 8% more than Cyril.)
