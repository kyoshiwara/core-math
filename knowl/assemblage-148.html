Problem-Solving Hints.

  1. It is very important to specify precisely what the variable represents. You will have to write an equation about this variable, so you must have a clear idea of what it stands for.
    In particular, the variable must stand for a number, so in the previous exercise, \(w\) stands for "Lima's weight," not for "Lima."
  2. Although the equation includes the variable, the two sides of the equation may actually be expressions for some other quantity.
    In the previous Example, the variable \(C\) stands for calories, but the equation gives two ways to express a ratio.
