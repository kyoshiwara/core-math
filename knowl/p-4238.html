  1. We can estimate the area of a circle by counting the number of squares that fit inside.

    1. On the circle on the left, put a dot in each square that is completely within the circle. Count the number of squares with dots.

    2. The circle on the right is enclosed in a square. What is the area of that square?

      The area of the circle is smaller than square units.

    1. On the circle on the left, put a dot in each square that is completely within the circle. Count the number of squares with dots.

      The area of the circle is larger than square units.

    2. The circle on the right is enclosed in a square. What is the area of that square?

      The area of the circle is smaller than square units.
